Introduction

The clinical trials for osimertinib, a drug approved for use in various stages of NSCLC (Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer) treatment.

  1. Drug Information:
    • Osimertinib is approved for use as an adjuvant, first-line, and second-line treatment in NSCLC (Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer) patients with either EGFR TKI sensitizing mutations or EGFR T790M resistance mutations.
  2. Clinical Trials Data:
    • AURA3 Clinical Trial (2nd Line Treatment):
      • Note: Comparison between osimertinib and platinum-pemetrexed.
      • Results: Median progression-free survival for osimertinib was 10.1 months (95% CI: 8.3–12.3), compared to 4.4 months (95% CI: 4.2–5.6) for platinum-pemetrexed.
      • Hazard Ratio for Disease Progression or Death: 0.30 (95% CI, 0.23–0.41), P<0.001.
    • FLAURA Clinical Trial (1st Line Treatment):
      • Note: Comparison between osimertinib and standard EGFR-TKI.
      • Results: Median progression-free survival for osimertinib was 18.9 months (95% CI: 15.2–21.4), compared to 10.2 months (95% CI: 9.6–11.1) for standard EGFR-TKI.
      • Hazard Ratio for Disease Progression or Death: 0.46 (95% CI, 0.37–0.57), P<0.001.
  3. Additional Information:
    • “Progression to osimertinib (EGFR inhibition) is still a challenge for many patients.”
    • The data cited in the graphs are from the New England Journal of Medicine, published in 2017 and 2018 respectively.

Disease Progression and Drug Resistance

  1. Expansion of Innate Resistance Clone
  • Initial Situation: Cells exhibit pre-existing heterogeneity which fosters resistance.
  • Response to Treatment: Maximum therapeutic response is initially observed.
  • Progression: Resistance clone expands, leading to disease progression.
  • Reactivity to Drug Withdrawal and Rechallenge: Cells are not responsive to drug withdrawal and rechallenge.
  1. Acquired Resistance from Drug Tolerant Persister (DTP) Cells
  • Initial Situation: Drug-tolerant ‘persister’ cells generate resistant clones.
  • Response to Treatment: Maximum therapeutic response is initially observed.
  • Development of Resistance: De novo resistance from DTPs emerges.
  • Reactivity to Drug Withdrawal and Rechallenge: Cells are responsive to drug withdrawal and rechallenge.
  1. Note
  • Interrelation of Mechanisms: These mechanisms are not mutually exclusive, implying they may coexist or interact in contributing to resistance in patients.

Examining osimertinib drug tolerant persister (DTP) cells in four EGFR mutant NSCLC cell lines using RNA-seq and ATAC-seq to identify common gene regulatory changes

  1. Overview:
  • Objective: To understand common gene regulatory changes in osimertinib DTP cells through RNA-seq and ATAC-seq analyses.
  1. Experimental Design:
  • Cell Lines Used:
    • PC9: Exon 19 deletion (del.)
    • NCI-H1975: L858R/T790M mutations
    • HCC827: Exon 19 del.
    • HCC2935: Exon 19 del.
  • Treatment Plan:
    • Cells are treated with DMSO, followed by acute osimertinib treatment for 24 hours. Further experiments include 3 weeks of osimertinib treatment to establish DTPs, followed by a short washout (24 hours) and a long washout (~10 days).
  1. Results:
  • EGFR Pathway Analysis:
    • EGFR pathway suppression in DTPs was validated both after acute and long-term treatment setups.
    • Western Blot Analysis: Showed protein levels for phospho-EGFR, EGFR, phospho-p42/p44 (indicative of MAPK activation), and p42/p44 across different time points during and after osimertinib exposure.

      • Phospho-EGFR Levels:

      • A decrease in phosphorylated EGFR is generally observed following osimertinib treatment, indicating effective inhibition of EGFR activation.

      • Upon washout, there appears to be a reactivation or rebound in phospho-EGFR levels in some samples, suggesting some level of resistance or adaptation by the cancer cells that allows them to reactivate EGFR signaling even after the removal of the drug.

      • Total EGFR Levels:

      • These remain relatively constant across treatments and washouts, indicating that the drug affects the activation state but not the expression level of EGFR.

      • Phospho-p42/p44 and p42/p44 Levels:

      • Changes in phospho-p42/p44 reflect alterations in downstream MAPK signaling following EGFR activation/inhibition. The observed variations in phosphorylation levels post-treatment can provide insights into how signal transduction adapts or is disrupted by osimertinib.

      • The total levels of p42/p44 also seem to remain consistent, similar to total EGFR levels.

  1. Insights:
  • The use of acute and washout periods helps in observing the dynamic response of DTPs to osimertinib, particularly how these cells reactivate signaling pathways after drug withdrawal. This might provide insights into the mechanisms behind the persistence and potential resistance of cancer cells to treatment.

Gene expression in osimertinib drug tolerant persister (DTP) cells across four EGFR mutant NSCLC cell lines using RNA-seq

1.Experimental Design (same design):

  • Cell Lines and Mutations:
    • PC9: Exon 19 deletion
    • NCI-H1975: L858R/T790M mutations
    • HCC827: Exon 19 deletion
    • HCC2935: Exon 19 deletion
  • Treatment Regimen:
    • Cells were treated with DMSO (control), followed by osimertinib for 24 hours (acute), then continuously for 3 weeks to establish DTPs. This was followed by a short washout (24 hours) and a long washout (~10 days).

  1. Observations:

    A. RNA-seq Data Visualization:

    • The gene expression here is Z-scored exprssion, across different treatment conditions and time points.
    • Clusters of genes (1-4) are identified, with a focus on clusters 1 and 3.

    B. Gene Clusters and Pathway Analysis:

    • Cluster 1: Related to cell-cycle processes.
    • Cluster 3: Related to epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT).

    C. Gene Expression Patterns:

    • Some gene expression changes induced by osimertinib treatment in DTP cells are slow to recover even during the long washout period, suggesting durable cellular adaptations or alterations.
    • The persistence of EMT-related gene changes is particularly noteworthy as EMT is linked to increased cancer cell invasiveness and resistance to therapies.

The impact of acute treatment and the persistence of effects in drug-tolerant persister (DTP) cells, along with recovery during washout periods

Heat Map:

  1. Suppression of DNA Replication Genes:
    • Acute osimertinib treatment significantly suppresses the expression of many cell cycle and DNA replication genes compared to DMSO.
    • In DTP cells, some genes still show suppressed expression but to a lesser extent than in acute treatment, suggesting partial adaptation or resistance to the drug’s effects on these pathways.
  2. Recovery During Washout:
    • During the short washout period, the expression levels of many of these genes begin to return towards baseline (DMSO treatment levels), indicating some recovery of normal cell cycle functions.
    • The long washout period shows a further normalization of gene expression, although some genes still display altered expression levels, suggesting lasting effects of osimertinib exposure.
  3. Gene Focus:
  • POL (DNA polymerase family members)
  • MCM (Minichromosome maintenance complex components)
  • RFC (Replication factor C)
  • LIG (DNA ligase)
  • PCNA (Proliferating cell nuclear antigen)

Osimertinib DTPs versus acute treatment Comparison (GSVA)

  1. Top Pathways Identified:
    • Extracellular matrix (ECM) secretion signatures
    • SMAD signatures
    • YAP1 signatures
  2. GSVA (Gene Set Variation Analysis):
  • The variation of pathway activity across the samples.
  1. Comparison of ECM-related pathway activity using GSVA:
  • A significant increase in ECM-related activities is noted in DTP cells compared to acute treatment, suggesting that DTP cells may employ ECM remodeling as a mechanism to survive drug treatment and possibly contribute to drug resistance.

(Summary so far) Osimertinib drug-tolerant persister (DTP) cells exhibit significant changes in gene expression related to extracellular matrix (ECM) secretion and related signaling pathways compared to cells treated acutely.
