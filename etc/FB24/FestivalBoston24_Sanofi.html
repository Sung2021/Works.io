Xenium Multimodal Cell Segmentation

Key components of this technology include:

  • Nucleus Segmentation: This initial step involves detecting nuclei using DAPI images with a custom neural network trained on a variety of tissue types.
  • Cell Segmentation: Following nucleus segmentation, the algorithm employs three methods to define cell boundaries. These include using cell boundary stains to identify true cell membrane boundaries, expanding from the nucleus to the cell interior stain edge, and a nuclear expansion method.
  • Multimodal Approach: This involves the application of different staining methods to better define and differentiate cellular structures within the sample. The segmentation algorithm adjusts its approach based on the stain used, offering flexibility in handling diverse tissue preparations, including fresh frozen and FFPE samples.

Understanding Tumor Microenvironment requires deep cell stage profiling

  1. Breast Cancer Prevalence and Mortality:
    • Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women in 157 countries.
    • It caused approximately 670,000 deaths in 2022 according to the World Health Organization (WHO).
  2. Components of the Tumor Microenvironment (TME):
    • Cancer Associated Fibroblasts (CAFs) and Tumor Associated Macrophages (TAMs) are major components of the TME.
  3. Role and Complexity:
    • CAFs and TAMs play a critical role in cancer progression.
    • Their heterogeneity and spatial distribution within the tumor microenvironment are poorly understood.
  4. Current Technological Limitations:
    • Antibody-based spatial technologies are not suited for detailed characterization of CAFs and TAMs.
  5. Proposed Solution:
    • Performing spatial transcriptomic analysis of the TME is suggested as a more effective method to understand these components deeply.
  6. Visual Representation:
    • The accompanying diagram illustrates interactions within the TME, showing the movement of Tregs and suppressive myeloid cells towards the tumor and highlighting the direct effects on Treg suppression.

M2-like Macrophages are predominant in ER+ breast cancer

Executive Summary

  1. Objective:
    • To investigate the diversity of Cancer Associated Fibroblasts (CAF) and Tumor Associated Macrophages (TAM) at the single cell level and understand their contribution to the immunosuppressive tumor microenvironment (TME) in ER+ breast cancer.
  2. Methodology:
    • Utilizing the Xenium in situ spatial transcriptomic platform to explore the heterogeneity of CAF and TAM populations.
  3. Key Findings:
    • Cellular Recapitulation: The study analyzed approximately 1.2 million cells, identifying 7 major cell types including about 50% tumor cells, 15% TAMs, 10% CAFs, and 8% T cells.
    • Immunosuppressive TME: There are complex and heterogeneous immunosuppressive environments, marked by distinct transcriptional signatures for CD8 T, CD4 T cells, and Tregs.
    • CAF Subtypes: Identified three major subtypes of CAFs (myCAFs, iCAFs, apCAFs) with myCAFs being predominant.
    • TAM Subtypes: Identified 10 subtypes of TAMs, with M2-like macrophages being predominant.
    • Spatial Relationships: CD8+ T-cell enriched regions are in proximity to myCAFs/iCAFs/M2-like macrophages but distal from the tumor, suggesting a regulatory role of these cells in T-cell infiltration into the tumor.
  4. Implications for Therapeutic Strategies:
    • The spatial transcriptomic analysis provides valuable insights into cell heterogeneity and the spatial context of these cells, offering a foundation to establish comprehensive models of the TME and potentially design new therapeutic strategies.
