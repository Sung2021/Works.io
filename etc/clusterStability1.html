SC3 - consensus clustering of single-cell RNA-Seq data (Method)

Single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq) enables a quantitative cell-type characterisation based on global transcriptome profiles. We present Single-Cell Consensus Clustering (SC3), a user-friendly tool for unsupervised clustering which achieves high accuracy and robustness by combining multiple clustering solutions through a consensus approach. We demonstrate that SC3 is capable of identifying subclones based on the transcriptomes from neoplastic cells collected from patients.

Multiple clustering algorithms test (s13059-022-02622-0.pdf)

(Note: a simple summary, this section will be updated)

Intra- and Inter-Cluster Similarity

To measure the closeness within clusters and the separation between clusters:
- scLCA: the Silhouette index.
- CIDR: the Calinski-Harabasz (CH) index.
- SHARP: both the Silhouette and CH indices, plus hierarchical clustering.
- RaceID: the Gap statistic.
- SINCERA: a minimum distance approach.

Community Detection-Based Techniques

To optimize community structures to identify clusters:
- ACTIONet
- Monocle3
- Seurat

Eigenvector-Based Techniques

Eigenvector-Based Techniques to determine the optimal number of clusters:
- SIMLR: Maximizes the eigengap.
- Spectrum: Applies a multimodality gap heuristic.
- SC3: Analyzes eigenvalues using the Tracy-Widom test.

Stability Metrics

To assesses the robustness of clustering results by considering the stability of clusters:
- densityCut: Models density of cell distributions to generate a hierarchical cluster tree and selects the most stable clusters.

Fig5

Impact of imbalance of cell ratios on number of cell type estimation and clustering

Background : most scRNA-seq experiments generate data that capture cell types with different number of cells, some- times with highly imbalanced ratios

How to create imbalance : Tabula Muris data to create major and minor cell types (each with 5 or 10 cell types) with imbalanced cell ratios of 2:1, 4:1, and 10:1 (see the “Methods” section for details)

Impact on Estimation of Cell Type Numbers

  • Decrease with Increasing Ratios: Imbalance in cell ratios tends to decrease the estimated number of cell types.
  • Overestimating Methods: methods like Seurat, SC3, ACTIONet.
  • Underestimation: methods like scLCA, scCCESS-Kmeans, scCCESS-SIMLR negatively.

Impact on Cell Clustering

  • Concordance Variability: Performance does not consistently match estimation accuracy; Seurat shows lower concordance.

Influence of Dataset Characteristics

  • Key Driver - Number of Cell Types: Significantly influences algorithm performance, especially in datasets with 10 and 20 cell types.
